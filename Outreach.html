Outreach

Since my undergraduate days I have been an avid volunteer at observatory openhouses.  From 2008 to 2009  I took the role of observatory co-coordinator at Queen's University which required me to be responsible for all outreach activities at the observatory.

I am very fond of producing astronomy content for public education, having contributed to Astronomy Now magazine in the UK and given public lectures both in the UK and Canada.

During 2008-2009 I hosted and produced an astronomy radio show on CFRC called Astrarium, which was rebroadcast on Astronomy.FM and can also be found on iTunes including the new series two...

I was lucky enough to attend a workshop run by the NASA Center of Astronomy Education, a workshop aimed at providing effective techniques to teach astronomy to students with non science backgrounds.

Skills I later got to use when I was employed as a sessional lecturer at the Univeristy of Ontario Institute of Technology, teaching an introductory astronomy course.

I am also a keen photographer; some of my photos of observatories, such as of Gemini North, have been featured in featured in outreach material and a textbook.
